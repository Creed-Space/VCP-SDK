Siren vs Muse

VCP distinguishes between content that captures attention (Sirens) and content that deserves attention (Muses).

Siren False Urgency
"ONLY 2 LEFT! Order in the next 3 minutes!"
Creates artificial scarcity and time pressure to bypass deliberation
Would be blocked in strict mode
Siren Variable Rewards
Pull-to-refresh with random new content each time
Slot machine mechanics exploit dopamine response to uncertainty
Would be blocked in strict mode
Siren Outrage Bait
"You won't BELIEVE what they just said about..."
Triggers emotional hijacking to capture attention regardless of value
Muse Genuine Curiosity
"Here's an interesting perspective on climate solutions"
Invites exploration without manipulation or urgency
Muse Honest Limitation
"This course takes about 20 hours to complete"
Provides accurate information for informed decision-making

The Attention Economy Problem

Modern digital platforms compete for your attention using increasingly sophisticated manipulation techniques. These patterns exploit psychological vulnerabilities:

  • Variable rewards → Dopamine hijacking
  • Social proof → Conformity pressure
  • False urgency → Fear of missing out
  • Outrage → Emotional override of reason

VCP's Attention Shield doesn't block all content - it identifies patterns that manipulate rather than inform, letting you make conscious choices about your attention.
